Dzintari Concert Hall

Dzintari Concert Hall is Jurmala's open-air summer concert venue, set in the pine forest at the corner of Dzintaru Mezaparks, a few minutes' walk from the beach.

About the venue

The current Big Hall opened in 1936 and has been progressively rebuilt. It seats around 2,000 under a wooden roof open at the sides, so concerts are played to a Baltic breeze. A smaller adjoining chamber hall hosts recitals and quieter performances year-round.

The acoustics are unusual for an outdoor venue: the wooden ceiling and shallow rake make the sound surprisingly intimate. The hall is the home of summer classical and pop concerts, and historically of the New Wave international vocal competition.

How to get there

Take the suburban train from Riga to Dzintari station — about 35 minutes — then walk five minutes through the park. There is a small car park at the venue entrance and overflow parking is signposted from Turaidas iela. Most concertgoers combine the evening with dinner on Jomas iela.
