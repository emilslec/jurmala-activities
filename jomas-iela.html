Jomas iela

Jomas iela is the pedestrian street that runs through the centre of Majori, two blocks back from the beach. It is roughly a kilometre long and is the densest concentration of restaurants, cafés and shops in Jurmala.

What's there

The street has been pedestrianised since the late 1990s. Along its length you will find Latvian restaurants, sushi places, pizzerias, beer gardens, ice cream stands, gift shops and the occasional bookshop. Live music, street performers and small art markets appear in summer evenings.

Where to eat

For a quick meal, the kiosks at either end of the street sell pelmeni, blinis and Latvian pastries. For a sit-down lunch, the side terraces of the larger restaurants are pleasant in summer. Prices are higher than central Riga during peak season.

Getting there

Jomas iela starts a short walk from the Majori suburban train station and ends near the Dzintari boundary. From the beach, walk inland on any of the side streets between Jomas and the seafront — they are all about 200 metres apart.
