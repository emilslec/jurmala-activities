Activities in Jurmala

Jurmala is a 33-kilometre strip of seaside towns on Latvia's Baltic coast, half an hour from Riga. This small guide collects the activities most visitors actually do.

The town is built around the beach — fine white quartz sand, shallow water, pine dunes — but there is more to it than swimming. There is a forest park behind the dunes, an open-air museum of old Latvian fishing huts, the country's largest water park, a pedestrian street full of cafés, and a summer concert hall that hosts the biggest names of the season. Each page below covers one of these in more detail.

Where to start

  • The beach and sea — the main attraction, and the page with the most detail. Start here.
  • Dzintaru Mezaparks — pine forest park with a viewing tower and rope course, just behind the dunes.
  • Jurmala Open-Air Museum — restored 19th-century fishing village with boats, nets and net-houses.
  • Livu Akvaparks — indoor and outdoor water park; the obvious rainy-day fallback.
  • Jomas iela — pedestrian street with restaurants, ice cream and street performers.
  • Dzintari Concert Hall — open-air summer concert venue in the pines.

How to get here

Jurmala is reachable from Riga by suburban train (about 30 minutes from Rigas Centrala stacija to Majori or Dzintari), by bus, or by car along the A10. There is no entry fee in summer for pedestrians; cars need a one-day pass to drive into the resort zone.

When to come

Peak season is mid-June through August, when sea temperatures climb into the low twenties Celsius. The shoulder months (May, September) are quieter and the beach is at its best for walking. Winter is cold and most beach amenities are closed, but the pine forest and concert hall remain worth a visit.
