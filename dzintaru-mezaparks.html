Dzintaru Mezaparks

Dzintaru Mezaparks is a small forest park directly behind the Dzintari section of the Jurmala beach. It is the most-visited of the city's parks and a natural pairing with a morning at the sea.

What it is

The park covers about ten hectares of mature Scots pine on stabilised dunes. There are gravelled walking paths, a children's playground, a wooden boardwalk linking it to the beach, and two paid attractions: a 38-metre observation tower and a rope adventure course. Entrance to the park itself is free.

The viewing tower

The tower has a spiral staircase up the inside and a viewing platform at the top with a clear view over the pine canopy and out to the Baltic. On a clear day you can see all the way along the coast to the river Lielupe and inland over the rooftops of the resort. There is a small fee to climb.

Rope course

The rope course consists of three difficulty levels strung between the pines, ending in a zip line. It runs from May to October, weather permitting. Children's harnesses are available.

Walking the park

The simplest visit is a 30-minute loop on the gravelled main path. Longer walks branch off into the dunes and connect to the beach at the Dzintari station exit. The park is dog-friendly on a leash.
